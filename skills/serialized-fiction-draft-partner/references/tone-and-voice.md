# Tone And Voice

Match the local prose temperature of the existing work, especially the immediately previous episode.

## Check

- density of narration
- sharpness or dryness of dialogue
- amount of sentiment
- amount of overt explanation
- how often inner monologue appears
- how harsh or lyrical the prose is allowed to become

## Preserve

- the established emotional temperature
- character-specific speech habits
- the balance between scene and exposition

## Avoid

- making the prose generically warmer or cooler than adjacent episodes
- making dialogue unnaturally explanatory
- making characters more articulate or self-aware than they have earned
- reusing the exact same emotional beat as the prior episode

## If Unsure

Bias toward the immediately previous episode's tone over a broader abstract idea of the series.
